# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your bio and display name professional.** Future employers, internship coordinators, and professors may search for you online. A clean, real-name profile creates a positive first impression.

2. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or a future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings actively.** Review who can see your posts, stories, and tagged photos. Set non-professional content to "Friends only" or "Close Friends" rather than "Public."

4. **Credit others' work.** When sharing memes, articles, photos, or creative content made by someone else, tag or credit the original creator. This is both ethical and legally safer.

5. **Use social media to learn and network.** Follow industry leaders, researchers, and companies relevant to your field. LinkedIn, Twitter/X, and Reddit have active communities for almost every engineering domain.

6. **Report and block accounts that harass or threaten you.** Every major platform has a reporting mechanism. You do not have to tolerate cyberbullying or unsolicited contact.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share your personal details publicly.** Your phone number, home address, college ID, Aadhaar number, and bank details should never appear in any public post, bio, or story.

2. **Don't post during exams, classes, or sensitive events.** Posting during college exams or sensitive institutional events can be misinterpreted and create unnecessary controversy.

3. **Don't engage with or share unverified news.** Forwarding misinformation — even unintentionally — can damage your credibility. Always verify a story with two independent sources before sharing.

4. **Don't use college or institutional names to express personal opinions.** Statements like "As a VIT student, I believe..." can unintentionally appear to represent the institution. Keep personal opinions clearly personal.

5. **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers without scrutiny.** Fake profiles are commonly used for phishing, catfishing, and social engineering attacks. Check for mutual connections and a realistic profile history before accepting.

6. **Don't post photos that include others without their permission.** Always ask before tagging friends, batchmates, or faculty in photos. What is harmless to you may be unwanted exposure for someone else.
