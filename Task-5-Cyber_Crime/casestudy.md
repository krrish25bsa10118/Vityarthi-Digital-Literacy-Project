# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud
**Classification:** Financial Cybercrime

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing forms of cybercrime in India. According to the Indian Cybercrime Coordination Centre (I4C), financial fraud accounts for over 67% of all cybercrime complaints filed on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal. College students — who are new to managing their own finances and are increasingly UPI-dependent — are a primary target group.

---

## Realistic Case Study: The "Wrong Transfer" Scam

**Scenario (Fictional but based on documented fraud patterns):**

Rohan, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, received a call one evening from a man claiming to be "Vijay from SBI customer care." Vijay said a customer had accidentally transferred ₹5,000 to Rohan's UPI ID instead of their own number, and the bank needed Rohan to "return the amount" to resolve the complaint.

To make it believable, Vijay sent Rohan a fake bank SMS (using a spoofed sender ID) that appeared to show a credit of ₹5,000. Rohan, seeing the SMS, believed the story.

Vijay then shared a UPI payment request for ₹5,000 and told Rohan to "enter your PIN to approve the reversal." Rohan entered his PIN — and immediately lost ₹5,000 from his own account. There had been no credit. The SMS was fake. The "approval" was actually a payment authorisation.

---

## Step-by-Step: How It Happens

1. **Hook:** Fraudster calls the victim claiming to be a bank official, courier company, or government authority.
2. **Fake Proof:** A spoofed SMS or screenshot "confirms" a bogus credit or refund.
3. **Urgency:** Victim is told the transaction must be reversed immediately or their account will be blocked.
4. **The Trap:** Fraudster sends a *collect request* or payment link. Victim enters their UPI PIN thinking they are receiving money.
5. **Loss:** The PIN authorises an outgoing payment. Money is gone within seconds.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted

- First-time UPI users (new college students away from home)
- People who have recently listed items for sale on OLX or similar platforms
- Anyone who has publicly displayed their UPI ID or phone number
- Elderly users, but increasingly young adults between 18–25

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (typically ₹2,000–₹50,000)
- Funds are rarely recoverable once transferred
- Emotional distress, shame, and reluctance to report
- In some cases, victims are manipulated into multiple transactions
- Loss of trust in digital payment systems
