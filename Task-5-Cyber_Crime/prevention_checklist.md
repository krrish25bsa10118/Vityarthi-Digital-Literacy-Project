# Stay Safe Online — Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## 🛡️ General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   Never reuse a password. Use a mix of uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. A password manager (like Bitwarden — free) can help you manage them.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   This includes your email, LinkedIn, GitHub, and any banking app. Even if someone steals your password, 2FA blocks them from logging in.

3. **Never click on links in unexpected SMS messages, emails, or WhatsApp messages.**
   Phishing links are designed to look official. Always open websites by typing the URL directly into your browser rather than clicking a forwarded link.

4. **Verify before you trust.**
   If someone calls claiming to be from your bank, IRCTC, or any government agency — hang up and call the official number listed on the organisation's website to verify the claim.

5. **Keep your phone's OS and apps updated.**
   Security patches close known vulnerabilities. Outdated apps are a common entry point for malware.

6. **Be cautious about public Wi-Fi.**
   Avoid accessing banking apps or entering passwords on public Wi-Fi at cafes, railway stations, or college canteens. Use your mobile data for sensitive tasks, or use a VPN.

---

## 💰 UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Remember: Entering your UPI PIN sends money — it never receives it.**
   You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to accept a payment, cashback, or refund. Anyone who asks you to "enter your PIN to receive money" is a fraudster. Full stop.

8. **Never share OTPs, UPI PINs, or CVV numbers with anyone.**
   Legitimate bank officials, UPI apps, and government agencies will NEVER ask for your OTP, PIN, or card details over a call or message. Not ever. Not for any reason.

---

## 📱 Social Media & Personal Data Safety

9. **Do not display your phone number or UPI ID publicly.**
   Avoid putting your number in public social media bios or marketplace listings. Use a secondary number for public listings if needed.

10. **Regularly review which apps have access to your contacts, camera, and location.**
    Go to your phone's Settings > App Permissions and revoke access that seems unnecessary. A calculator app does not need your contacts.

---

## 🚨 If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (24×7, toll-free) |
| **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** | **cybercrime.gov.in** |
| **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Available on the back of your debit/credit card |
| **Local Police Cybercell** | Visit your nearest police station and ask for the cybercrime desk |

> ⚠️ **Report within the first hour of a UPI fraud.** Banks can sometimes block or reverse transactions if the report reaches them quickly. Every minute counts.
